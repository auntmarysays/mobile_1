"There are many factors affecting outcomes for both the conventional CPR and the eCPR patient.

Age is definitely a factor when looking at survival numbers. Those between the ages of 18 and 74 had the best survival. Downtime without blood flow is another important variable when predicting outcomes. The pathology, or reason for arrest, is also important. While sex has been evaluated, it does not appear to be a factor in survival when using conventional or extracorporeal CPR.
